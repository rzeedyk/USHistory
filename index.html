The Boston Tea Party

It's December 16, 1773. You find yourself in Boston Harbor, where tensions are high due to unfair taxation. A group of colonists are planning to dump British tea into the harbor as a form of protest.

What do you choose to do?

Joining the Boston Tea Party

You decide to join the protesters. Disguised as a Mohawk Indian, you board the ship and start throwing tea overboard. The splash of the tea crates hitting the water is oddly satisfying.

What's your next move?

Warning the British

You rush to warn the British about the impending protest. They appreciate the information but seem unsure how to respond. Your loyalist leanings may have consequences...

What will you do now?

Paul Revere's Midnight Ride

It's April 18, 1775. You're Paul Revere, and you've just found out the British are coming to arrest Samuel Adams and John Hancock. It's like finding out the school principal is on their way to bust the most popular kids for planning a massive food fight!

You need to warn everyone, but how? No smartphones, no social media... just you, a horse, and a whole lot of determination.

The Declaration of Independence

It's the summer of 1776 in Philadelphia. The Continental Congress is meeting, and there's more drama than a season finale of "Stranger Things." You're part of the committee tasked with drafting the Declaration of Independence.

Thomas Jefferson is hunched over his desk, quill in hand, looking like he's about to drop the hottest track of 1776. But he's stuck on the opening lines.

Jefferson turns to you and asks, "How should we start this revolutionary document?"

The Battle of Trenton

It's December 25, 1776. General Washington has a daring plan to cross the Delaware River and surprise the Hessian forces in Trenton. It's so cold, you feel like you're in an episode of "Game of Thrones" - winter is definitely coming!

As you prepare to cross the icy river, you overhear soldiers discussing their doubts. What do you do?

Valley Forge

It's the winter of 1777-1778, and you're at Valley Forge with Washington's army. The conditions are brutal - it's colder than a White Walker's handshake, and supplies are scarcer than toilet paper in a pandemic.

Morale is low, and soldiers are considering desertion. What action do you take?
